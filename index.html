Mate Tasting

Local Traditionals

We'll have an introduction of the history of this unique drink that it is considered a cultural heritage. We´ll be learning about the Guaranies (native people in some South American countries) who really worked to exploit the benefits of yerba mate and who perfected the technique and process, by placing the leaves in a gourd with water and sipping the liquid through straws they made from sugar cane. Also we are going to talk about the “gauchos” (a kind of Argentine cowboy) settled down throughout Argentina. They were known for their independent, rural and semi-nomadic way of life. These skillful riders took the tradition of drinking mate from the Guaranies. You are going to prepare your own "mate" drink, learning also typical words and “the codes of the mate”. You will learn how to prepare it yourself and will sit back, sipping mate and of course, make a round, the same way as Argentineans do every time they gather.

REQUIRED LEVEL: A2 to C1

Many travelers around the world come to visit these fantastic ruins at the top of the mountain and are impressed by the beauty that surrounds it. We will visit the main sections of this amazing citadel while we practice our Spanish.

World Heritage

Iguazu Falls are one of the most impressive natural landscapes in the world. On this tour, we'll explore these spectacular waterfalls and their surroundings. Las Vegas

City Tour

Enjoy a virtual tour along Fremont street in the historic downtown Las Vegas. Coined as Old Vegas, Fremont was the first paved street in Vegas in 1925 and is still home to the oldest casinos including the infamous Golden Nugget and its resident shark and The Golden Gate Hotel. We will start out at some fantastic vintage casinos and the Caesars Palace hotel: one of the most iconic hotels in Las Vegas. We will show you the most historic, impressive, and quirky sights in the city. Visit to the “Welcome to Fabulous Las Vegas” sign; famous the world over and visible in countless TV and film scenes, this classic piece of Americana is even on the State Register of Historic Places. Your guide will narrate the architectural details of famous hotels and resorts as you explore the city. You’ll see the gleaming exteriors of the Venetian, Caesars Palace, Mandalay Bay, Mirage, Aria, Wynn, and Paris Las Vegas.
